I took a train with Shani from Paddington on Thursday. I arrived way too early, of course, but still only bought the ticket very last minute. The different ticket types are very confusing in a new country. The train only had 5 coaches and we barely had a place to sit. We spent the journey talking and reading. The train station in Oxford is a very big mess. It is one of the busiest stations in England relative to its size, and positioned outside of the city center. Very few buses go there, and Shani spent around 10 minutes complaining about it. On Friday, Shani and Cai had to work, so I had the day to myself. I went up and down the canal and walked around the city. [Add Shani's maps? removing the house arrows?] I kept making wrong turns on the southern part, as there are not many bridges between the two sides of the rivers, and in some places one of the river banks is blocked, so I had to turn back a few times. On Saturday Shani and I went to the East Oxford Farmers' Market. We had amazing Samosas at Pindys, bought homemade yogurt and smoked fish. We also bought three apple juice bottles from Tiddly Pommes; Emparor Alexander (my favourite, very sharp and sour), Winston (I think. Also very good) and Discovy (amazing but my least favourite as it was sweet). All the apples are collected from around Oxford, from old trees, and made into juice without mixing varieties, so you can taste the differences. We then walked around Oxford, buying many vegtables and a few snacks to try. We maded boiled and baked soft yam, green mango pickles and pickled daikon and cucumbers in soy sauce and mirin. We also cooked snake gourd scramble eggs! On Sunday, we went to the city center. We got amazing bubble tea at Formosan. They only have 4 tea options (black tea or oolong tea, with milk or with lemon) which is always a good sign. I got black tea with lemon, with pearls and of course less sugar as consistancy is a normative value. The Bodleian Libraries had an exhibition about pets (and a variety of Tolkien Merch), and we met two friends of Shani there, who joined us punting. We then went to M&S to get drinks and berries for the punting. Half of the food side was missing so the fruits and vegtables were between the clothes, and the drinks at room temperature. Cai also came punting with us, and all five of us had a great time. It was much harder than I thought, especially to not crush into the river bank or into other boats in shallow water. In the evening we then went to Orient Cafe, an amazing Sichuan restaurant. Even though I was ignored in my request to remove the coriander from all the dishes, everything was amazing. My stomache ache from all the oil started at the restaurant itself, so I spent the second half of the meal paring everything with rice, which helped, but it was so worth it. The tofu was so crunchy, the eggplants so sweet crispy, the crispy chicken and green beans so addictive, and the boiled fish in chilly oil and Sichuan peppers was probably my favourite. Mulan update:
